What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks. These attacks often aim to access, change, or destroy sensitive information; extort money from users; or interrupt normal business processes. It is essential for everyone—from individuals to large organizations—to understand and apply basic security measures to stay safe online.

Essential Cybersecurity Tips

  • Use strong, unique passwords for every account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) where available.
  • Keep your operating system and software up to date.
  • Be cautious of suspicious emails and links—phishing is common.
  • Back up your data regularly to secure, offline storage.
  • Install antivirus software and keep it updated.

Common Cyber Threats

Some common cyber threats include:

  • Phishing: Fake emails or websites designed to steal your personal information.
  • Ransomware: Malicious software that locks your data until a ransom is paid.
  • Malware: Software designed to harm or exploit any programmable device or network.
  • Spyware: Programs that secretly monitor user activity and collect information.
  • Social Engineering: Tactics that trick people into giving up confidential information.
